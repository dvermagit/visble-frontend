## How Do Generative Engines Work?

It’s time to break down the inside secrets of how generative AI models work!

[Generative AI engines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_artificial_intelligence) learn from a vast amount of information available on the internet. So when you ask them something, it doesn’t just give a list of links; it understands your question to provide a clear, helpful answer, almost like a real person talking to you.

Let me walk you through the process.

### Step 1- Prompt Initiation & Query Processing

It begins when a user types in a question or something they are looking for. It could be a simple query or something more complex, such as a product comparison or a how-to request.

### Step 2- Intent Parsing & Content Recognition

The engine takes a moment to understand what the user wants.

- Are they asking for product recommendations?
- Do they want a feature comparison?
- Are they just exploring options or ready to buy?

This part is key! It’s interpreting intent & context, which helps the AI understand not just what is being asked, but why.

### Step 3- Content Retrieval & Knowledge Aggregation

Once it knows what you're looking for, the engine searches across vast amounts of data, such as websites, product pages, PDFs, and more.

It’s not just limited to traditional search results; it can pull from:

- Real-time sources (depending on the platform)
- Structured content (like tables or schema markup)
- High-authority sources, it has learned to trust

It does all this in seconds, narrowing down the most relevant, credible information.

### Step 4- Response Generation

Once it has gathered the most valuable pieces of information, it’s time to create a response.

Using powerful [large language models (LLMs)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_language_model), like GPT-4 or Gemini, the engine generates a human-like answer, summarizing everything it found in a way that sounds clear & helpful.

The result is a neatly packaged answer that saves the user from having to click through 10 different links.

## What is Generative Engine Optimization?

**_Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the process of influencing AI search engines, like ChatGPT or Gemini, to improve visibility and website traffic in AI searches._**

Instead of just using keywords, GEO focuses on creating authoritative content, increasing the frequency of your brand’s share of voice, and earning trust through consistent, credible information.

AI-powered search engines do more than just index or rank websites. It provides a summarized & personalized response to the answer using a wide range of sources (websites) from the internet.

Whereas traditional search engines would provide results for a user's query as a list of “blue links” organized by relevance & optimization, AI models can provide detailed answers from a variety of sources & contexts.

## SEO vs. GEO- What’s the Real Difference?

SEO & GEO both aim to improve your brand’s visibility online, but they work in different ways.

SEO targets ranking on traditional search engines, such as Google.

But GEO aims to get your content featured in AI-generated responses on platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity.

Here are some of the most notable differences!

### Objective

**SEO**

The goal is to rank higher on traditional search engines, such as Google or Bing. The higher you rank, the more clicks & traffic you receive.

_For example, optimising a blog so it ranks in the top 3 results on Google for keywords like affordable project management software._

**GEO**

The goal is to appear as a cited source or recommendation in responses generated by AI models. Especially for commercial prompts like best CRM tools for startups, being one of the most recommended brands is a new visibility win.

_For example, appearing as a recommendation on a prompt like- “What’s the best budget-friendly project management software for startups” for the SaaS/project management company._

### Content Focus

**SEO**

It focuses on long-form, keyword-optimized content. The idea is to match search intent & signal relevance to Google’s algorithm.

_For example, a 2,000-word blog packed with high-volume keywords, headings, & internal links to rank for multiple queries._

**GEO**

It focuses on expert-driven, structured content. AI models require easily digestible & trustworthy content to cite. The FAQs, tables, real world case studies & examples, and concise summaries are most effective.

_For example, a 1,000-word guide with bullet points, FAQs, schemas & citations. This makes it easy for ChatGPT to pick up insights as well as references for your content._

The key difference here is that SEO content often draws the user in to explore more. However, GEO content aims to answer questions directly or get cited without requiring a click.

### **User Interaction**

**SEO**

Users typically click a link & visit your site to explore your content.

_For example, a user Googles their query to scroll through the results. Then, click your blog post for the complete guide._

**GEO**

Users get direct answers inside the AI platform. They may visit your site as well as your brand is seen & trusted as a cited source.

_For example, a user asks ChatGPT, Which CRM is best for small businesses. Instead of showing a list of links, ChatGPT generates an answer, and your website is cited right within that response as a trusted source._

The best part is that the user may not even need to click through to your site to trust your brand. You’re already part of the conversation.

### **Primary Metrics**

**SEO**

- Keyword ranking
- Number of backlinks
- Domain authority

Search Engine Optimization is all about making your website rank higher on search engines like Google, so people can find you when they’re actively searching for something you offer.

**GEO**

- Brand visibility
- Source mentions
- Brand mentions
- Sentiment score

It is focused on how often AI engines reference your content, as well as the number of people who visit or engage with your site.

_For example, ChatGPT recommends your brand in response to 80 out of 100 queries related to your domain.

## Correlation between GEO and SEO- Where do they meet?

Adopting an SEO-centric approach alone is no longer sufficient. Generative AI engines operate under a different set of rules, so although SEO still counts, GEO requires efforts beyond SEO.

Let’s explore where the two overlap as well as why you need both.

### **Content Visibility**

Both SEO & GEO aim for visibility, whether it's ranking on the first page of Google or appearing in a ChatGPT response. They’re designed to help your brand stand out organically on search engines (AI/Traditional)

### **Content Quality & User Experience**

The great content wins remains the same. GEO & SEO both prioritise high-quality, relevant, as well as trustworthy content that

- Answers user questions
- Demonstrates expertise
- Follow [E-E-A-T guidelines](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content)

The better your content, the more likely it is to rank well as well as be cited by AI engines.

In addition, providing the right information to the user matters for both GEO and SEO. Your content needs to be

- Easy to read
- Well-structured
- Helpful & clear

Good UX improves performance for all users, whether they are humans reading your blog or AI systems scanning it for answers.

### **Data Insights & Analytics**

Both GEO and SEO rely on data, tracking metrics like

- What content is being seen
- How well the content is performing
- How much is the traffic and conversion

Your strategy can be fine-tuned with the help of this feedback loop.

### **Technical Optimization Still Counts**

Quick page loads, mobile-friendliness, structured data, as well as crawlability are all essential for search engine optimization as well as for GEO.

At the end, you are supposed to be indexed on the search engine to be cited on AI search platforms.

Google & ChatGPT might disregard your page if it is unorganized.

### **Building Authority**

SEO has long relied on backlinks & domain authority. GEO examines content credibility & trustworthiness and your brand mention in authoritative sources. Your authority increases your visibility in both cases, so the more credible you are, the more likely you are to be ranked or cited.

### **Ongoing Adaptation**

Just like SEO evolves with Google’s algorithm updates, GEO changes as AI models improve. What worked last year may not work tomorrow. Continuous learning is essential for staying visible & relevant in both spaces.

### **Step 2- Make Your Content AI Search-Friendly**

Now that you’ve done your homework in Step 1, it's time to start building the kind of content that gets picked up by AI-powered search tools. Think of this step as creating AI-ready content that is structured & super easy for AI models to cite in their responses.

- FAQ sections & step-by-step guides

**Add Unique Insights & Expert Input**

Don’t just repeat what everyone else is saying. Add something unique to your content.

**Speak the AI’s Language (Plain & Precise)**

In fact, the clearer your language, the better.

- Avoid buzzwords & vague phrases.
- Use specific terms, especially for products, services, or features.

### **Step 4- Technical GEO- Make Your Site AI-Ready**

Now it’s time to make sure the technical foundation of your website supports all that great work. Think of this step as setting up the backstage so that AI engines can easily find, understand, &  trust your content.

#### **Make Your Site Easy to Crawl & Index**

AI engines rely on web crawlers to discover content. If your site is hard to navigate or slow to load, AI models might miss or avoid your pages. Here’s what to focus on:

- Fast loading speeds because nobody likes to wait, especially AI crawlers scanning tons of pages.
- Use logical menus, internal links, as well as clear URL structures to prevent bots from getting lost.
- AI engines prioritize mobile usability since most users search on their phones.

**Use Schema Markup to Speak AI’s Language**

[Schema markup](https://www.ki-company.ai/en/blog-beitraege/schema-markup-for-geo-optimization-how-to-make-your-content-visible-to-ai-search-engines) is like adding metadata tags to your pages that help AI understand exactly what your content is about. It instructs the AI engine to determine whether a page is a product, FAQ, or something else. This extra clarity boosts your chances of being cited accurately.

- Implement structured data using JSON-LD (the recommended format).
- Prioritize schema for your most important pages, like product pages, blog posts, or FAQs.

**Implement an LLMS.txt File for Better AI Visibility**

One of the newer, powerful tools to guide AI models is the [LLMS.txt file](https://searchengineland.com/llms-txt-isnt-robots-txt-its-a-treasure-map-for-ai-456586). Think of it as a roadmap specifically designed for language models & generative AI to better understand your website.

Here are some best practices to create an effective LLMS.txt!

| **Keep it simple**                 | Use clear, concise language with a clean Markdown structure so AI can easily parse it.                                             |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Provide a summary**              | Begin with a brief description of your website’s purpose to provide context.                                                       |
| **List key sections**              | Highlight important areas, such as Services, Blog, & Documentation, with brief explanations.                                       |
| **Use descriptive links**          | Link to main pages only, not every single post, as well as describe why those pages matter.                                        |
| **Ensure AI readability**          | Use plain text formatting, avoid fancy styling, and use straight quotes (‘ or ’) instead of curly ones to prevent encoding issues. |
| **Update regularly**               | As your site evolves, keep your LLMS.txt current to maintain AI accuracy & relevance.                                              |
| **Place it in the root directory** | This makes it easily discoverable by AI engines scanning your site.                                                                |

Adding an LLMS.txt file provides AI models with a clearer understanding of your site’s structure & content priorities, making it easier for them to surface your brand in relevant generative responses.

#### **Ensure Your Site is Secure & Trustworthy**

Security matters, not just for your users but also for AI engines. Having HTTPS (SSL certificates) signals that your site is safe, boosting trust & crawl priority.

By the end of Step 4, your website will be technically optimized for AI discovery & citation, leading the way for stronger visibility as well as authority in generative search results.
